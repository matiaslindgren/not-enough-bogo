Is this the most useless app in the universe?

Well it is certainly not the most useful one.

The randomized bogosort algorithm has an unbounded worst-case time complexity, which means the amount of time required to sort a sequence with the algorithm is unknown. It is not difficult to come up with examples which essentially work in the same way as the randomized bogosort algorithm. Lets say you would like to play Chopin's Etude Op.25 No.9 but you have never practiced the piece. Lets say there is only one sequence of notes which is considered the 'correct' way to play the piece. Finding this sequence using a bogosort algorithm would work as follows:

  1. Press random piano keys.
  2. If the piece was Chopin's Etude Op.25 No.9, stop playing.
  3. Else, continue from 1.

This ridiculousness may rightly be dismissed as inefficiency but I find all kinds of ubiquitous, dadaistic stupidity, due to its casual and non-belligerent nature, indeed quite calming. Moreover, the simple thought of optimizing a multi-pivot, in-place quicksort algorithm, while sobbingly tinkering with 9 different C++ pointers due to off-by-one errors makes me want to stop living and start a new life as a panda baby, just rolling around and gnawing on interesting objects. Besides, this random shuffle of 10 000 elements looks pretty damn awesome, don't you think?
